Chromosome 1
Chromosome 1 contains 2000-2100 genes that affect proteins. Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: 1p36 deletion syndrome, 1q21.1 microdeletion, 1q21.1 microduplication, neuroblastoma, thrombocytopenia-absent radius syndrome, and a variety of cancers and chromosomal conditions.
Chromosome 2
Chromosome 2 contains 1200-1300 genes that affect proteins. Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: 2q37 deletion syndrome, MBD5-associated neurodevelopmental disorder, SATB2-associated syndrome, and a variety of chromosomal conditions. Somatic mutations in chromosome 2 have also been associated with a variety of cancers impacting blood and bone marrow cells.
Chromosome 3
Chromosome 3 contains 1000-1100 genes that affect proteins. Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: 3p deletion syndrome, 3q29 microdeletion syndrome, 3q29 microduplication syndrome, clear cell renal carcinoma, and a variety of chromosomal conditions.
Chromosome 4
Chromosome 4 contains 1000-1100 genes that affect proteins. Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: facioscapulohumeral muscular dystrophy, PDGFRA-associated chronic eosinophilic leukemia, Wolf-Hirschhorn syndrome, and a variety of chromosomal conditions. Chromosome 5
Chromosome 5 contains roughly 900 genes that affect proteins. Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: 5q minus syndrome, 5q31.3 microdeletion syndrome, Cri-du-chat syndrome, PDGFRB-associated chronic eosinophilic leukemia, periventricular heterotopia, and a variety of cancers and chromosomal conditions.
Chromosome 6
Chromosome 6 contains 1000-1100 genes that affect proteins. Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: 6q24-related transient neonatal diabetes mellitus and a variety of chromosomal conditions. Somatic mutations in chromosome 6 have also been associated with a variety of cancers.
Chromosome 7
Chromosome 7 contains 900-1000 genes that affect proteins. Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: 7q11.23 duplication syndrome, FOXP2-related speech and language disorder, Greig cephalopolysyndactyly syndrome, Russell-Silver syndrome, Saethre-Chotzen syndrome, Williams syndrome, and a variety of chromosomal conditions. Somatic mutations in chromosome 7 have also been associated with a variety of cancers, notably leukemia.

Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: 8p11 myeloproliferative syndrome, core binding factor acute myeloid leukemia, recombinant 8 syndrome, trichorhinophalangeal syndrome type II, and a variety of cancers and chromosomal conditions.

Chromosome 9 contains 800-900 genes that affect proteins. Mutations in this chromosome could potentially cause: 9q22.3 microdeletion, bladder cancer, chronic myeloid leukemia, Kleefstra syndrome, and a variety of cancers and chromosomal conditions.
